# Project Rhizome

**A time-aware, conflict-native knowledge graph designed to preserve epistemic texture, intellectual lineage, and contested ideas.**  
**Pilot domain:** Quantum Mechanics (Many-Worlds Interpretation).

---

## Strategic Mandate

Project Rhizome is an epistemological intervention against information flattening.

Most modern knowledge systems optimize for fast consensus: smooth summaries, compressed narratives, and “best answer” retrieval. This hides the actual process of knowledge formation—debate, methodological conflict, historical reversals, and unresolved tensions.

Rhizome is built to do the opposite.

It models knowledge as a **living, contested network** rather than a finished hierarchy.  
The goal is not passive consumption, but active cultivation: users become **gardeners** of ideas, exploring how concepts emerge, clash, decay, and return.

---

## Constitutional Principles (Non-Negotiable)

1. **Knowledge as a Network, Not a Tree**  
   Favor cycles and multi-parent structures over rigid taxonomies.

2. **Conflict as Data**  
   Disagreement is first-class structure, not noise to be removed.

3. **Context is Queen**  
   Every node carries methodological provenance and epistemic flavor.

4. **Serendipity as the Metric**  
   Optimize for reframing and non-obvious conceptual neighbors.

5. **Time is Texture**  
   Preserve birth, decay, dismissal, and resurgence through temporal versioning.

6. **User as Gardener**  
   Humans participate in curation through transparent, accountable interventions.

7. **Depth Over Breadth**  
   A deeply mapped pilot domain beats shallow global coverage.

## System Architecture: Four Engines

### Engine 1 — Ontological Engine
Graph foundation with typed nodes/edges for:
- intellectual development (`PROPOSES`, `REFINES`, `EXTENDS`)
- critique (`CONTESTS`, `REFUTES`, `CRITIQUES`)
- temporal dynamics (`RESURRECTS`, `ABANDONS`, `SUPERSEDES`)
- lateral discovery (`RESONATES_WITH`)
- governance transparency (`WEIGHTED_BY`)

Includes edge-level timestamps and historical snapshots.

### Engine 2 — Curation & Integrity Engine
Stake-weighted governance with tiered source confidence:
- Tier 1: Bedrock
- Tier 2: Commentary
- Tier 3: Fringe

Supports pruning, grafting, community anchors, and visible weighting logic.

### Engine 3 — Traversal & UX Engine
Progressive disclosure:
- Layer 0: Consensus frame with explicit contestation
- Layer 1: Lineage + methodology + temporal context
- Layer 2: Full rhizomatic texture

Pathfinding uses methodology friction to make conceptual boundary-crossing explicit.

### Engine 4 — Discovery & Justice Engine
Pattern-mining for hidden structure:
- structural resonance across domains
- “dead ends that weren’t” resurrection signals
- citation equity and under-recognition analysis

## Pilot Guardrail (Strict Scope Lock)

**This repository is locked to the Many-Worlds Interpretation (MWI) pilot domain.**

No expansion beyond Quantum Mechanics until the pilot satisfies success criteria:

- 100 users pass the 15-minute reframing test
- 20+ users report genuinely non-obvious discoveries
- 60% reframing rate is reached

If these metrics are not met, scope expansion is prohibited.

---

## Definition of Success

Rhizome is successful only if users can quickly move from “answer retrieval” to **epistemic reframing**—understanding not just *what* ideas are, but *how* and *why* they became accepted, rejected, or revived.

**Maintain texture. Do not flatten.**
